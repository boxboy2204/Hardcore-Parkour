1\. The Physics & Gameplay Engine

* Genre: 2D Side-Scrolling Auto-Runner with high-fidelity 16-bit pixel art.  
* The "Hardcore" Mechanic: You must tap the "PARKOUR\!" button within a 0.2-second window upon landing.  
  * Success: A gold "Hardcore\!" text pops up, granting a speed boost and building your "Viral Video" style multiplier.  
  * Failure: A white "Oof" appears, the character stumbles, and the multiplier resets.  
* Character Stats:  
  * Michael: Balanced speed; highest "Style" gain per shout.  
  * Dwight: Heavy landing; can break through weak floorboards to find shortcuts.  
  * Andy: "A Cappella" Leap; features a double-jump but a larger "Injury" hitbox.

2\. Level Selection (The Conference Room Meeting)

* The Interface: A 2D view of the Conference Room. All levels are Polaroid photos taped to a messy, marker-scribbled whiteboard.  
* The Interaction: Your active runner stands by the board. Michael spins a marker, Dwight scans for threats, or Andy tunes a banjo.  
* The Dialogue: They pester you to choose (e.g., Michael: "Pick the Warehouse\! I want to show Darryl my soul\!").  
* The Launch: Tap a Polaroid and Michael slams a gavel on the table, shouting: "Meeting Adjourned\!"

3\. The Shop (The Break Room & Jim’s Quest)

* The Merchant: Jim Halpert leans against the vending machine. All upgrades (shoes, shields, chili-pots) are encased in thick orange Jell-O.  
* Jim’s Side Quest (The Unlock): You cannot buy anything initially. Jim tells you Pam is lost in the Warehouse.  
  * You must complete a 2D Stealth-Platformer mission in the Warehouse, dodging Darryl’s line of sight to find Pam.  
  * Once found, she gives you Jim’s Desk Key, which unlocks the top row of the machine.  
* The Forehead Stare: If you try to pay in Schrute Bucks, Jim locks eyes with your character’s forehead in a 100% silent, psychological standoff.  
  * The Escape: You can only break the stare by leaving in shame OR by handing over the correct amount of Stanley Nickels. Once paid, Jim smirks and finally "de-gels" the item.

4\. The Dressing Room (The Annex Boutique)

* The Stylist: Kelly Kapoor manages your outfits, which are earned via Dundie Achievements.  
* The Economy: Kelly only accepts Schrute Bucks.  
* Kelly’s Side Comments: Kelly provides real-time feedback as you toggle clothes:  
  * The Cornell Fit (Andy Only): "Omigosh, Andy, you look like a mascot for a school I’ve definitely heard of\!"  
  * Ryan's Beard: "You look like a bad boy who just returned from a corporate retreat... I love it. Here's 500 Schrute Bucks\!"  
  * Date Mike: "You look like you're about to lose all your money at a pool hall. It’s so vintage."  
  * The Wrong Fit: "Is that a sumo suit? In New York? That is so 'ironic-chic,' I literally can't even."  
  * Recyclops (Dwight only): *"*Is this for Burning Man? Because Ryan said we might go to Burning Man if he sells enough WUPHF stock, but honestly, you look like a transformer that got stuck in a dumpster. It’s totally 'Eco-Terrorist Chic,' and I’m kind of obsessed*?"*

5\. World Progression & Secrets

* The Worlds:  
  1. The Bullpen: Jumping desks and dodging Angela’s projectile cats.  
  2. The Warehouse: Navigating conveyor belts and the "Find Pam" stealth mission.  
  3. Scranton Streets: Running on the roof of the Work Bus and dodging Jim’s snowballs.  
  4. NYC Corporate: Vertical elevator climbs and dodging Jan’s flying folders.  
  5. The Final Pursuit: A high-speed highway chase across car roofs.  
* The Secret Warp: Equip the Goldenface Suit from Jim’s desk, enter the Conference Room, and touch the "World's Best Boss" mug to enter the 8-bit Threat Level Midnight world as Michael Scarn.

6\. The Loading Screen & Pause Menu

* The Loading Animation: In the bottom right, pixel-art Dwight and Andy are in a continuous loop leap-frogging over each other while the progress bar fills.  
* The Audio: A rhythmic 8-bit "Hup\! Hup\! Hardcore\!" chant.  
* The Performance Review (Pause Menu): Two options: CONTINUE or QUIT.  
  * The Quotes: A random Famous Office Quote is displayed next to the options (e.g., "I'm not superstitious, but I am a little stitious.").

7\. The Finale (The Strangler)

* The Boss: A car-hopping chase to unmask the Scranton Strangler, who is always revealed as Toby.  
* The Celebration: A 2026 pixel-art concert featuring Michael singing "Goodbye Toby" (Remix).  
* The Final Burn: A jail-cell cutscene where Michael’s voice crackles over the intercom: "Toby Flenderson, report to the principal's office. Your mother called... you wet the bed again."

8\. Technical "Boss" Features

* The "Boss is Coming" Button (PC Only): Hit F12 to swap the game for a fake Excel spreadsheet titled "Paper Distribution Q1."

9\. End Screen

* David Wallace gives a star rating out of 5 depending on how well you did in the level  
* The higher rating you get the more Shrute bucks and Stanley nickels you get  
* This is kind of like a “Performance Review” at the end of each level.  
* If you get a 1 star rating, David just looks at the screen and says, “I am going to need to see you on saturday.”

10\. Secret Achievements (Dundies)

* You can view these in the Annex Boutique, displayed on a shelf behind Kelly.  
* The "Hottest in the Office" Award: Collect 1,000 "Paper Ream" pickups in the Warehouse.  
* The "Whitest Sneakers" Award: Successfully hit 50 "Hardcore\!" landings in a row without scuffing your shoes.  
* The "Don't Go In There" Award: Accidentally jump into Kevin’s spilled chili 3 times in one run.
